## Post Type 1 — Single Deal

**Structure:**
```

[Product Name / Offer Name]
[Optional: MRP and discount %, or deal conditions]
[Link(s)]
```

**Media:** Optional product image (photo). CashKaro attaches product card images. DesiDime uses hcti.io-generated deal card images. FreeKaaMaal and CouponzGuru attach product photos.

- DesiDime: always "Read More - [link] / Buy Now - [link]"

## Post Type 6 — Campaign Burst

**Purpose:** Saturate the channel with multiple posts about the same sale event over a short window (1–5 hours) to maximize reach, catch users at different times, and test which angle drives more clicks. Each post varies the headline, discount claim, or product focus while keeping the same destination sale.

**Structure:** Repeated posts sharing:
- Identical campaign name / headline
- Rotating discount claim or angle per post
- Unique affiliate tracking link per post
- Consistent footer

**Media:** Varies per post in the burst — some with image, some text-only.

**CTA:** Varies per post within the burst.

**Typical Length:** 3–7 lines per post; 5–12 posts in a burst window

**Typical Frequency:** Triggered by specific sale events; 2–5 campaign bursts per month at major channels

**Evidence:**

## Key Structural Observations

**1. Every major channel uses Single Deal as its primary volume driver.**  
No exceptions. The difference is in how the single deal is formatted: DesiDime is most stripped-down (% off + name + two links); CouponzGuru adds branded images; CashKaro adds a fixed cashback upsell footer on every post; GrabOn (2026) adds structured emoji templates.

**2. Collections are a differentiation tool, not a universal format.**  
GrabOn uses Price Bucket Collections as its signature format in 2026. CouponzGuru and CouponDunia use Brand/Category Collections. DesiDime uses none — every post is a single deal.

**3. Coupon Code Deals are exclusive to channels with brand partnerships.**  
CouponzGuru clearly has commercial relationships with brands (Snitch, Titan, Lenovo, PUMA, Ajio) evidenced by channel-branded promo codes. DesiDime, CashKaro, and FreeKaaMaal do not appear to have this.

**4. Bank Offer as a sub-type, not standalone.**  
Bank offers (HDFC, SBI, ICICI) almost always appear as an addendum to another deal type, not as their own post. They are a modifier, not a primary post type.

**5. Cashback appears in three distinct forms:**  
(a) Platform cashback offer (DesiDime — CRED, Bajaj, UPI offers);  
(b) Channel cashback upsell (CashKaro — bot forward footer on every post);  
(c) Third-party cashback stack (FreeKaaMaal — FKM Cashback rate shown per post).

**6. Campaign Burst is GrabOn's unique differentiator vs. competitors.**  
No competitor channel was observed doing this — posting 9 variants of the same sale over 3 hours with unique tracking links per post. CouponzGuru does a looser version (11 Ajio posts in 1 hour) but without the identical repeated headline formula.

**7. Polls, Forwards, and Videos are negligible or invisible in public data.**  
These may exist but are either rare, disabled in channel settings, or not rendered in the public t.me/s/ preview used for this research.
